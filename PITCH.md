# PITCH — Say It Out Loud

*The kid-simple pitch for investors. Memorize the bold lines.*

---

## 🍋 The 30-second pitch (explain it like I'm 8)

> **Imagine you want to post cool stuff on the internet, but staring at an empty page is scary and hard.**
>
> **Other helpers hand you a blank page and say "write something amazing!" — too hard.**
>
> **We hand you a recipe book of stuff that *already worked*. You tell us one little thing about you — like "I make videos about puppies" — and POOF, out comes a whole week of ready-to-post stuff: the words AND the pictures. Done.**
>
> **It's free. But every free thing we make has a tiny sticker that says "Made with us." So when your friends see your cool posts, they see the sticker and they come too. We grow just by people *using* us — no ads needed.**
>
> **When you love it and want it faster, prettier, and with no sticker, you pay us a little every month. That's how we make money.**
>
> **And the best part: it barely costs us anything, because the smart robots that do the thinking are the ones people *already have*. We just hand them the recipe.**

---

## 🚪 The one-liner (for the door / the elevator)

> **"We're the recipe book and the kitchen for the internet's creators — free to taste, pay to feast, and every dish is a tiny ad for us."**

---

## 🔢 The three numbers to drop right after

1. **$1M ARR = ~3,300 people paying $25/mo.** Small to reach.
2. **Our AI cost is ~$0** — free users run it on their own AI; paying users fund their own.
3. **Every user brings the next one for free** — built-in virality, ~$0 customer acquisition.

> Investor translation: *grows itself + costs almost nothing to run.*

---

## ❓ If they ask "why won't Jasper/Canva crush you?"

> **"They hand people a blank box. Blank-box AI makes generic stuff the apps now *hide*. We start from what already worked — and every free post we make is an ad that brings the next user. They have to buy growth; we get it for free."**

## ❓ If they ask "what's the moat?"

> **"Three things that compound: (1) the biggest library of *proven* content recipes, (2) a viral mark on every output so we grow at near-zero cost, and (3) memory — over time we learn each creator's voice and what works for them, so leaving means starting over."**

## ❓ If they ask "how big can this get?"

> **"Today: a recipe book. In a few years: an agent that runs your whole content — every platform, every language — while you sleep. The market's already paying for that; we get there from a free funnel nobody else has."**

---

## 🏪 The marketplace upside (Phase 3 — the "it spins faster" part)

> **"Later, the best creators put *their* winning recipes in our book and sell them — and we take a slice of every sale. More recipes makes our book the best book, which brings more creators, which makes it better. It's the App Store, but for content that's proven to work."**

Why it's powerful: creators stock our shelves for free, the "proven" moat deepens, and we add a two-sided network + new revenue — *after* we already have a crowd to sell to.

---

## 🎬 The demo that closes it

Show, don't tell: **type one line → a full week of content appears in 8 seconds.** One jaw-dropping before/after clip is worth more than any slide. (This is how Submagic seeded its first 40–50 customers and hit $1M ARR in 90 days.)
